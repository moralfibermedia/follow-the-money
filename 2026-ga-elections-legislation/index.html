📰 The Paper Ballot Standoff

The biggest story of the session: the legislature failed to pass a paper ballot bill — and the fallout could reshape the November election.

Senate version (HB 960): Mandatory hand-marked paper ballots by the November 2026 general election. Partisan bill. Passed the Senate — the House never took it up.
House version (SB 214): Paper ballots as a "permissive" means of conducting elections. Bipartisan. Passed the House late on Sine Die — the Senate never took it up.
Both bills: Would have removed QR codes from ballots.
The consequence: A 2024 law (SB 189) springs into effect — requiring that, by July 1 of this year, the text portion of paper ballots marked and printed by electronic ballot markers be the official vote, with QR codes no longer used. There is strong consensus that the SoS and county boards of election cannot be in a position to comply with this law by July 1.
Important clarifier: SB 189 does not mandate paper ballots. It only changes which part of the ballot is the official vote.
⚠ EXPECT: Litigation, confusion, and perhaps a special session. By the time this is resolved, it is very likely that the November election will be conducted using the current system.
