📖 Plain Language

Sine Die

SIGH-nee DIE-ee · Latin: "without a day"

The official end of a legislative session — adjourning without setting a date to reconvene. Georgia hits Sine Die every year around late March or early April. After Sine Die in year one, unfinished bills are paused but stay alive; they pick up where they left off the following January.

End of Biennium

bye-EN-ee-um · the two-year legislative cycle

Happens at Sine Die of year two. At this point, every unfinished bill dies for good. Slate wiped clean. Anything a legislator still wants must be re-introduced from scratch in the next biennium with a brand-new bill number.

April 2026 was the worse kind of Sine Die — the gravedigger version. That's why these bills are accurately called dead, not stalled.
