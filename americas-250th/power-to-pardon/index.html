THE POWER TO PARDON

The Constitution · Article II
The Constitution hands one person the power to erase a federal crime. Match each rule to what it actually does.
SOURCES: U.S. CONSTITUTION · 28 CFR · DOJ
We do the research. You solve the puzzle.
The Constitution lets a president erase a federal crime with a signature — no judge, no jury, no appeal. There's exactly one hard limit written into the clause itself. Everything else is just norms.
