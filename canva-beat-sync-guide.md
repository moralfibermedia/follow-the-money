# Canva Beat Sync Video — Quick Reference

## Setup

1. Open Canva → **Create a design** → search "TikTok Video" (1080×1920) or "YouTube Short"
2. You get a blank vertical canvas with a timeline at the bottom

## Step 1: Screen Recording

Before opening Canva, record yourself playing a puzzle on your phone or laptop:
- **iPhone**: Settings → Control Center → add Screen Recording. Swipe down, tap record, play the puzzle, stop recording. AirDrop to your laptop.
- **Mac**: Cmd+Shift+5 → Record Selected Portion → frame your browser window
- Aim for 20-30 seconds of gameplay showing: tap, wrong guess (shake), correct match, confetti

## Step 2: Add Recording to Canvas

1. **Uploads** (left sidebar) → drag in your screen recording
2. Resize to fill the canvas (pinch/drag corners)
3. Trim the clip: click the video → drag the edges of the clip in the timeline to cut dead time
4. If the recording is landscape, crop to focus on the puzzle area — vertical framing matters

## Step 3: Add Music

1. Left sidebar → **Audio** → search for a track (try: "inspiring," "build," "minimal beat")
2. Drag the track onto the timeline below your video
3. **Beat Sync**: click the audio track → click **Beat Sync** (magic wand icon in the toolbar above the timeline)
4. Canva auto-detects beats and places markers on the timeline
5. Adjust if needed — you want beats to land on your text card transitions

## Step 4: Add Text Cards

1. Click **Text** → **Add a heading**
2. Style it: white text, black background bar, or your MFM style (Playfair Display if available, otherwise a bold serif)
3. Type your first card: "Every dollar you spend is a political act."
4. In the timeline, drag the text element to start at 0s and end at the first beat marker (~3s)
5. Repeat for each text card — snap them to beat markers so text changes land on the music

### Text Card Timing

| Time | Text | What's on screen |
|------|------|-----------------|
| 0-3s | Every dollar you spend is a political act. | Static puzzle index or dramatic pause |
| 3-6s | I built something to prove it. | Opening the puzzle |
| 6-10s | Three grocery stores. Real donation data. | Charts visible |
| 10-14s | Match the company to the cash. | Tapping a company tile |
| 14-17s | Nope. | Wrong guess, shake animation |
| 17-22s | Every answer links to the source. | Correct matches, maybe two in a row |
| 22-25s | Five puzzles. Sixty seconds. All free. | Confetti moment |
| 25-28s | follow-the-money-index.moralfibermedia.com | Hold on URL |

## Step 5: Animations

1. Click a text element → **Animate** (top toolbar)
2. Best options for text cards: **Pop**, **Fade**, **Rise**, or **Typewriter**
3. Keep it consistent — same animation on every card
4. Don't over-animate. One style, clean transitions.

## Step 6: Fine-Tune

- **Trim audio** to match video length (drag the end of the audio track)
- **Fade out** audio: click audio track → Volume → add fade out (1-2 seconds)
- **Watch it through** at least twice with sound off — does the text tell the story without audio?
- **Check text readability**: white text needs a dark background bar or drop shadow to read over video

## Step 7: Export

1. **Share** (top right) → **Download**
2. File type: **MP4 Video**
3. Quality: **1080p** (default is fine)
4. Download

## Step 8: Post

**TikTok**: Open TikTok → + → Upload → select video → add caption + hashtags → post
**YouTube Shorts**: YouTube app → + → Upload → select video → add title (under 60 chars) → set as Short → publish

### Captions/Hashtags

TikTok: `Every dollar you spend is a political act. Five free puzzles built on public data. Link in bio. #FollowTheMoney #CivicEducation #PoliticalDonations #OpenSecrets #MoralFiberMedia`

YouTube Short title: `Which grocery store is nearly purple? 🟣 #shorts`

## Pro Tips

- **6 words max per text card** — people scroll fast, dense text gets skipped
- **First 3 seconds decide everything** — hook or die
- **End on the URL** — hold it for 3+ seconds so people can read it
- **Post between 6-9am or 6-10pm** for best initial reach
- **Repost the same content across TikTok, YouTube Shorts, and Instagram Reels** — Canva's resize feature (Magic Switch → resize to Instagram Reel) handles format differences
- **One puzzle per video** — don't try to show all five. Each puzzle is a separate piece of content.
- **Make 5 videos from 5 puzzles** — that's a week of content from what you already built

## Canva Resize for Cross-Posting

After exporting for TikTok:
1. **Resize & Magic Switch** (top menu)
2. Select "YouTube Short" and "Instagram Reel"
3. Canva auto-adjusts — review and tweak text positioning
4. Export each format separately
